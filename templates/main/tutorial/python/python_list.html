Python List



Section 1.1: Python Data Type

In general, Python offers 6 data types to assign. They're: List, Set, Tuple, Dictionary, String, Numbers

In this section, we'll cover one of these: Python List

  • List - a collection of ordered elements.

  • List - mutable (meaning that we can change its elements).

  • List - elements start with an index of 0, not 1.

  • List - allows dublicated elements.

  • List - Square brackets [ ] are used to define list data type.

Section 1.2: List Example

List elements are separated by commas and closed by square brackets.

a  =  [ 1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 ]#     defining simple list
print ( a )#     1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678

b  =  [ 10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99 ]# defining numerical list
print ( b )#     10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]#     defining string list
print ( a )#     'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow'

Section 1.3: Accessing List items

List items can be accessed through index only. E.x: list [ index ]

list [ start : stop : step ]

a  =  [ 1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 ]#     index 0 is 1st element of a list
print ( a[0] )#    1

  • First element starts with index 0, not 1.

b  =  [ 10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99 ]#     show items with index 0 , 1 , and 2.
print ( b[0:3] )#     10 , 23.99 , 10.99

  • Show items starting with index 0 till 3 (not included).

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]#     show items with index 2 and 3.
print ( a[2:4] )#     'hen' , 'cat'

  • Show items starting with index 2 till 4 (not included).

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]#     -index means start counting from backside
print ( a[-1] )#     'cow' -> 1st index from backside

  • minus( - ) means start from backside.

  • E.x: [-1] -> last element, [-2] -> second element from the back.

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]#     [ a: ] means start from index 'a' till the end.
print ( a[1:] )#     'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow'

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]#     show items with index 2 and 3.
print ( a[2:4] )#     'hen' , 'cat'

Section 1.4: Combining 2 lists

  • merged_list  =  list1 + list2

a  =  [ 1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 ]
b  =  [ 10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99 ]
c  =  a  +  b#     merging 2 lists
print ( c )#     1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 , 10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99

Section 1.5: 'step' - 3rd argument of a list

b  =  [ 10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99 ]
print ( b[ : : 2 ] )#       10 , 10.99 , 0.99
print ( b[ : : 3 ] )#       10 , 0.56

Section 1.6: Reversing a string

b  =  [ 10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99 ]
print ( b[ : : -1 ] )#       0.99 , 0.56 , 10.99 , 23.99 , 10  ( reverse step )
print ( b.reverse(  ) )#       0.99 , 0.56 , 10.99 , 23.99 , 10  ( ready-made function )

Section 1.7: Changing List Item

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]
a [ 2 ]  =  'sheep'#       item with index 2 has been reassigned
print ( a )#       'puppy' , 'dog' , 'sheep' , 'cat' , 'cow'

Section 1.8: Built-in functions

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]
a.append ( 'sheep' )#       'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow', 'sheep'   (adds item from back)

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]
a.pop ( )#       'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat'   (removes last item if index is not specified)

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]
len ( a )#   5   (displays total number of items)

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]
a.insert ( 2 , 'sheep' )#       'puppy' , 'dog' , 'sheep' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow'   (insert item in a specified index)

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]
a.remove ( 'dog' )#       'puppy' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow'   (removes specified item)

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]
a.clear ( )#       removes all items

a  =  [ 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' ]
'cat'   in   a#       True
'cat'   not   in   a#       False

Section 1.9: Removing dublicated items

We can simply change data type of list to set and then convert it again to list.

a  =  [ 10 , 23.99 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 10, 0.56 , 0.56 , 0.99 ]
a  =  list ( set ( a ) )#       0.56 , 0.99 , 10 , 10.99 , 23.99

  • set ( ) automatically removes dublicated items.
