Python Set



Section 1.1: Python Data Type

In this section, we'll cover: Python Set

  • Set - a collection of unordered elements.

  • Set - don't support indexing.

  • Set - automatically removes dublicated items.

  • Set - curly brackets{ } are used to define sets.

Section 1.2: Set Example

Set elements are separated by commas (like lists) , but closed with curly brackets.

a  =  { 1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 }#     sets are unordered, hence an item order can change
print ( a )#     0.678 , 1 , 10.99 , 'cat' , 'dog'

b  =  { 10 , 23.99 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 10, 0.56 , 0.56 , 0.99 }#     sets automatically remove dublicated items
print ( b )#     0.56 , 0.99 , 10 , 10.99 , 23.99

a  =  { 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' }#     sets don't support indexing
print ( a[0] )#     Error

Section 1.3: Accessing Set items

1st way : Set items can be accessed through for loop. We'll learn more detailed later.

items  =  { 1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 }
for   item    in   items :
    print ( item )#    1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678


2nd way : Checking existence using in :

items  =  { 1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 }
if   'cat'   in   items :
    print ( 'Exists!' )

Section 1.4: Combining 2 sets (3 ways)

  • merged_set  =  set1  +  set2

a  =  { 1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 }
b  =  { 10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99 }
c  =  a  +  b#     merging 2 sets
print ( c )#     1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 , 10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99

  • set1.update(set2)

a  =  { 1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 }
b  =  { 10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99 }
a.update( b ) #     merging 2 sets
print ( a )#     1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 , 10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99

  • set1.union(set2)

a  =  { 1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 }
b  =  { 10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99 }
a.union( b ) #     merging 2 sets
print ( a )#     1 , 'dog' , 10.99 , 'cat' , 0.678 , 10 , 23.99 , 10.99 , 0.56 , 0.99

Section 2.1: Python Set Operations

In general, Python offers 4 operations over sets. They are: Union, Intersection, Difference, Symmetric Difference

a  =  { 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' }
b  =  { 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' }
a.union( b )#       'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' , 'dog' , 'puppy'

a  =  { 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' }
b  =  { 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' }
a.intersection( b )#     'hen' , 'cat' , 'puppy' , 'cow' , 'dog'

a  =  { 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' }
b  =  { 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' }
a.difference( b )#     'dog' , 'cow' , 'cat' , 'puppy' , 'hen'

a  =  { 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' }
b  =  { 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' }
a.symmetric_difference( b )#     'dog' , 'cat' , 'hen' , 'cow' , 'puppy'

Section 2.2: Built-in functions

a  =  { 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' }
a.add( 'sheep' )#     'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow', 'sheep'   (adds item)

a  =  { 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' }
a.pop( )#     'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat'   (removes last item)

a  =  { 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' }
len( a )#     5   (displays total number of items)

a  =  { 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' }
a.discard( 'hen' )#     'puppy' , 'dog' , 'cat' , 'cow'   (removes specified item)

a  =  { 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' }
a.remove( 'dog' )#     'puppy' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow'   (removes specified item)

a  =  { 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' }
a.clear( )#     removes all items

a  =  { 'puppy' , 'dog' , 'hen' , 'cat' , 'cow' }
'cat'   in   a#     True
'cat'   not   in   a#     False
